Getting started

Sign in or create your account

Gopher Go is an app-only, passwordless experience — nothing to memorize. You sign in with a one-time code sent to your phone.

  1. Download Gopher Go (free) and tap Sign in with phone.
  2. Enter your number and tap Send Code.
  3. Type the 6-digit code we text you. That's it — you're in.

New here?

Tap Create an account and add your name, phone, email, address, and date of birth. We'll verify your phone and email with the same quick code step.

↗
Were you referred? Add their codeIf another Gopher (or a friend already on the app) brought you here, there's an optional "Referral Info" step during sign-up — enter the Gopher ID of whoever referred you and you'll see their name pop up to confirm. It's optional and skippable, but adding it is how they get credit for referring you. Arrived by scanning someone's QR or link? It's filled in for you — just confirm.
✓
Use your legal first & last nameThis has to match the name on your bank account or it'll break your payout setup. Your name, birthday, and phone number can't be changed once saved, so double-check before tapping save. Email, address, and bio you can update anytime.

Complete your profile

A complete profile builds trust and helps customers pick you. From your dashboard, open Account → Personal Info.

Personal info

  • Add a recent, clear profile photo — real faces get hired.
  • Confirm your legal name, birthday, phone, email, and address.
  • Write a short bio that sells your skillset.

Ride-sharing & business info (optional)

  • Upload photos of completed jobs. Trust us on this one — a portfolio is one of the biggest drivers of getting requested by name.

Getting started

Set up your payout account

This is the one that unlocks everything. On your dashboard go to Account → Payout Account. A valid payout account is required to view and accept requests — no payout account, no jobs.

  • Payouts run through Stripe using a custom process. There are no fees to get paid.
  • Money is deposited directly to your bank the moment a job is confirmed complete (see payout speed below).
  • Add a debit card that's eligible for instant deposits so you're not waiting.
⚠️
Check your card before you acceptNever accept a request knowing your payout card needs updating — that's the #1 cause of a delayed payout. You can confirm your debit card supports instant deposits on Stripe's eligible-banks list.

Step 4 of 7 Offer types

Understand exactly what you'll make

Three offer types, three things to know. The number you pocket is always the Earn $.

Earn $

The straightforward one. The Earn $ amount is exactly what you make. Earn $20.00 means $20.00 lands in your account at completion.

Total $ (Earn $ | Spend $)

The customer added funds for you to buy items on their behalf. You front the Spend $, you keep the Earn $, and the Total $ is what's deposited at completion.

Earn $ (your pay)$10.00
Spend $ (reimbursed)$10.00
Total deposited at completion$20.00

Don't accept a Total $ job unless you're happy to cover the Spend $ up front.

Request bids from local workers

The customer didn't set a price — they asked local workers to send bids. You name your own number (your Earn $) and submit it. If they pick your bid, the job is yours at that Earn.

Step 5 of 7 Question, counter & accept

Decide and lock it in

  • Question? — Need more info before deciding? Tap Question? to message the customer. This is for clarifying the job, not for negotiating price or swapping personal contact info.
  • Counter-Offer — Want the job at a different number? Propose a counter on your Earn $ (your pay). If accepted, it's yours at the new amount — and the customer is bound to it. Keep it reasonable: a 3-mile, $5 trip might fairly counter to $10 — not $30.
  • Accept Request — All good? Agree to the cancellation terms box and tap Accept Request. You're on.
⚖
How many counter-offers, and how high?On Standard you get 5 counter-offers a month, and a counter can go up to 150% of the offer (minimum $20). Go Elite, Elite+, or Pro and counters are unlimited and uncapped — one of the bigger reasons to verify up.
🚩
Never take it off-platformAsking for payment, or swapping phone numbers / emails to deal off Gopher, is an instant removal. All messaging stays in the app to protect everyone's privacy. And quite honestly, there's no benefit to taking it off the app. Want to make $100? If a job says $100, that's exactly what you make — we don't take a cut.

After the job

Cost adjustments

Use a Cost Adjustment when the real price of purchased items differs from the customer's estimate. Simply submit the new amount and the customer can approve it immediately.

Example

The customer budgeted $10 for an item that rings up at $10.91. Submit a Cost Adjustment for the new cost of items — $10.91 — and upload the receipt. Done.

Best practices

  • Item costs are never earnings. If the estimate is off by more than $1 — over or under — adjust to the exact amount and attach the receipt. Put yourself in the customer's shoes; it's a no-brainer.
  • Big jumps? Adjust before you buy. If the new cost would raise the total request by more than 20%, submit the Cost Adjustment first so the customer's payment can cover it.
  • If a large adjustment can't be approved, let the customer know purchases must stay within their original budget.
🧾
Heads upA request can't be completed while a Cost Adjustment is still pending approval — so submit it promptly.
After the job

Age-restricted deliveries

For age-restricted items, the law and our policy are strict — and the customer already agreed to these terms when they ordered.

  • When you set the job to Completed, you must photograph the front of the customer's ID in the app before handing over the items.
  • Contactless delivery is never allowed for these orders.
  • Missing the ID step can mean a delayed payout or disqualification.

Customer not there?

You must be at the drop-off location. Tap "Tap here if customer is not present at drop off location…" to start a 10-minute timer. If they're still not there when it expires, select "Recipient No Show," leave with the items, and you're paid in full.

Good to know

Stay active & in good standing

A few habits keep your account healthy and your earnings flowing.

Avoid these cancellation reasons

  • "Too far" — distance is posted before you accept. Check it.
  • "Not enough money" — earnings are clear from the start.
  • "Didn't mean to accept" — that'd be a lot of checkboxes to click through by accident.
  • "Wasn't clear what to do" — DM the customer with Question? before committing.

Do this every time

  • Update your job status in real time, at the right location.
  • Complete jobs promptly and professionally.
  • Communicate respectfully — always.
