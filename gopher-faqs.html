Customer FAQs (Gopher Request)

Gopher Requests are available 24/7/365. If there's a Gopher requested and a Gopher available, we go to work!

Gopher charges the following fees to make a request:

  • Gopher Fees: This ranges from $0.99 to $4.99, depending on the type of service requested.
  • Instant Transfer Fee: 8% of the total request amount. This covers the operating cost to ensure the Gopher is paid immediately upon completing your request.
  • Age-Restricted Fee (if applicable): $1.99 covers the cost of the age-restricted safeguarding in place.
  • Gopher TrustShield Discount (for age-restricted deliveries): $1.00 is deducted from all age-restricted requests if you add the free Gopher TrustShield.

For example, a “Delivery – General Errand” where you want to pay a Gopher $30 total would cost $33.47 (Cost of Goods = $20, Gopher Offer = $10, Gopher Fee = $0.99, Instant Transfer Fee = $2.48). The Gopher receives all $30 of your offer, costing you only $3.47 to use the app.

Knowing the cost of the items you want, availability, and store hours is important because once the Gopher accepts the request, they're doing so in good faith that everything requested is available and priced accordingly. This lets you order what you need without costly markups. If the cost of goods submitted is a little off, your Gopher can submit a COST ADJUSTMENT for the correct amount. We just ask you to be as close as possible.

There are usually only a couple reasons a request isn't accepted:

  • We may not have enough Gophers in your area yet. Anytime a request is submitted in a new area, we get to work recruiting available Gophers there.
  • The offer may have been deemed too low for the Gophers in your area. Gopher is NOT an auto-fulfillment service — the offer you make is 100% of what your worker earns. They work for you, not Gopher, Inc. If your offer is below the typical accepted offer, you'll see a pop-up sharing the average offer for that category. You can still offer whatever amount you want.

Your request should be as descriptive as possible — the more info the better. No one knows what you want done better than you, so take your time describing EXACTLY what you want so there's no confusion.

Example of what not to do: “Need mulch spread in my backyard.” Example of what to do: “I need 12 yards of mulch laid in my backyard. Must have own equipment” (while supplying any relevant pictures).

We strongly recommend NOT putting your phone number in the request. We've provided the ability to communicate before and during your request through the app.

Anytime there's a “Cost of Goods” amount listed, the Requestor is paying you that amount to pay on their behalf. For example, for a meal from a Food Truck with no advance payment option, they'll enter the amount in Cost of Goods for you to pay on their behalf. This keeps costs down for everyone — Merchants, Requesters, and Gophers all benefit. If you aren't interested in these transactions or don't have the funds to cover the Cost of Goods, please DO NOT accept the request.

When a Gopher signs up, they receive their first 10 payouts at Stripe's “Standard” US payout speed (typically 2 hours from the time the customer confirms). After 10 successfully completed requests, the Gopher is upgraded to “Instant Payout” — payouts deposited immediately after completion (1–2 minutes). Gopher Elite members start with Instant Payout.

Payouts are initiated when a Gopher updates the request status to “Complete.” This triggers an SMS and app notification to the Requestor, who selects “Confirmed” or “Dispute.” When “Confirmed” is selected, the payout process starts and deposits go directly to the Debit Card on file. If the Requestor forgets to confirm, payout automatically occurs after 48 hours. As a best practice, remind the requestor in person to select confirmed right then to avoid delays.

In your app, tapping Account → Request History → Completed → View Request Logs shows the status of a payout, including Order Completed, Requester Confirmed, and Payout Completed timestamps.

Payouts can be delayed by: an expired, unsupported, compromised, bank-blocked, and/or cancelled card. Updating to a valid card resumes pending payouts, but causes a delay that can't be sped up — so ensure your card is in good standing before completing a request.

With Stripe's exceptional payout track record, a delay is easy to identify. Any time there's a delay or error:

  • Check the card used to ensure it's still valid (confirm with your bank it isn't blocked, compromised, and/or expired).
  • Add a new valid payout card to your Payout Account and set it as the default payout method.
  • Delete the old card (do this AFTER the new card is entered to save your Stripe payout history).

Once a valid card is established, Stripe confirms it the next business day, then existing payout amounts transfer to the newly confirmed card (also ~1 business day). Avoid this reconciliation if possible — there's no way to speed it up once the initial attempt fails, and contacting Gopher won't speed it up. This is easily avoided by keeping a valid card on file before completing a request. Rest assured, once you complete a request the money is always accounted for.
